Question 5:
What is your impression of the intended
primary goal of the interactive experience?

The impression I had about the website was that it was designed to act as a
digital portfolio.

Question 6:
How does the interactive experience
communicate this primary goal?

The website was quite successful in communicating its goal as a portfolio.
Rather than heavily focusing on various types of user interaction, the website
focused on aligning all contents through one major page, then guided the
viewer through the uniquely designed scrolling path, which seemed as if
I was viewing a slideshow.

Question 7:
What is your impression of how the
experience should be interacted with over time?
(For how long and how many different times)

I believe the scrolling feature of the main website was designed to be
experienced once as it creates a one-way linear path for the user to interact,
however, the webpage itself is designed to be accessed multiple times, as
the contents have their individual page which links back to the main
website.

Question 8:
How does the interactive experience
communicate how it should be interacted with
over time?

The interactive experience communicates that the webpage should be viewed
as an interactive presentation, with the user following the singular main path
while accessing the contents along the way, which displays more detailed
information.

Question 9:
What other media forms (digital or otherwise)
does the experience reference?

The experience references a portfolio, a presentation, a video and a notepad.

  • Portfolio - The website's purpose is to act as a portfolio, showcasing the creator's
    work.
  • Presentation - The website structure is designed like a presentation, with one
    path guiding the user's experience.
  • Video - There are many aspects of the website which contain and involve
    video, such as the background and the presenting media.
  • Notepad - The visuals of the website is designed in a whiteboard-like
    style with the cursor resembling a pen used for digital notes.

Question 12:
What is the most frustrating part of the
interaction to you and what makes that part
frustrating?

The most frustrating aspect of the website is the layout of the content pages and
redirection to the main page.

Firstly, the unique scrolling path of the main webpage has a complex movement
already, yet the sub content pages share a similar path design, turning the
webpage from a path into a maze.

Secondly, the subpage's scroll bar is located at the bottom, creating an
annoying experience for the user, needing to relocate the scroll bar
for faster travelling each time they switch between the pages.

Lastly, the redirection button to the previous page is unclear
and requires testing clicks, which often directs to
irrelevant pages and further confusing my
location within the complex
website.

What is the most satisfying part of the

satisfying?

The scrolling experience of the homepage and the following animated visuals
is the most satisfying part of the interaction. The website uses minimalistic
design with white text which directs the user's focus on the subjects with
motion. This also gives modern and professional values to the website
which is satisfying to view.
